Find the missing number in the following number series:
$$3, 7, 15, 31, 63, ?$$

A) 95
B) 111
C) 127
D) 128

### Solution
Let's analyze the difference pattern:
- $7 - 3 = 4$
- $15 - 7 = 8$
- $31 - 15 = 16$
- $63 - 31 = 32$
The difference is doubling each step: $4, 8, 16, 32$.
The next difference must be $32 \times 2 = 64$.
Hence, the missing number is:
$$63 + 64 = 127$$
Alternatively, the pattern follows $x_{n} = 2x_{n-1} + 1$:
$$2 \times 63 + 1 = 127$$

A and B can complete a piece of work in 10 days and 15 days respectively. If they work together, in how many days will they complete the same work?

A) 5 days
B) 6 days
C) 8 days
D) 9 days

### Solution
- Work rate of A = $\frac{1}{10}$ of work per day.
- Work rate of B = $\frac{1}{15}$ of work per day.
- Combined work rate = $\frac{1}{10} + \frac{1}{15} = \frac{3 + 2}{30} = \frac{5}{30} = \frac{1}{6}$ of work per day.
Hence, they will complete the work in 6 days.

A train running at the speed of 72 km/h crosses a pole in 15 seconds. What is the length of the train (in meters)?

A) 150 m
B) 200 m
C) 300 m
D) 450 m

### Solution
First, convert the speed of the train from km/h to m/s:
$$\text{Speed} = 72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 4 \times 5 = 20 \text{ m/s}$$
The distance traveled to cross the pole is equal to the length of the train:
$$\text{Length of train} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 20 \text{ m/s} \times 15 \text{ s} = 300 \text{ m}$$

A sum of money invested at simple interest doubles itself in 10 years. Calculate the simple interest (in Rupees) earned on a principal amount of Rs. 1000 over 10 years at the same rate of interest.

### Solution
- Let principal be $P$. Since the money doubles itself in 10 years, the Amount $A = 2P$.
- Interest earned $SI = A - P = P$.
- Therefore, the interest earned over 10 years is exactly equal to the principal amount.
- Given Principal = Rs. 1000, the simple interest earned over 10 years will be Rs. 1000.

A man walks 5 km towards South, then turns right and walks 3 km. He then turns left and walks 5 km. In which direction is he now with respect to his starting point?

A) West
B) South
C) North-West
D) South-West

### Solution
Let's track his movement from origin (0,0):
1. Walks 5 km South $\to$ reaches (0, -5).
2. Turns right (which is West when facing South) and walks 3 km $\to$ reaches (-3, -5).
3. Turns left (which is South when facing West) and walks 5 km $\to$ reaches (-3, -10).
His final position is (-3, -10), which is in the South-West quadrant with respect to the starting point (0,0).
