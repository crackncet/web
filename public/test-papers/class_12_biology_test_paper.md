Which of the following nitrogenous bases is present in RNA but absent in DNA?

A) Adenine
B) Thymine
C) Uracil
D) Cytosine

### Solution
Uracil is a pyrimidine nitrogenous base found in RNA (Ribonucleic Acid). In DNA, thymine is present instead of uracil. Adenine, guanine, and cytosine are found in both DNA and RNA.

What is the primary function of Restriction Endonucleases in genetic engineering?

A) To cleave double-stranded DNA at specific recognition sequences
B) To join separate fragments of DNA together
C) To replicate DNA strands during PCR amplification
D) To transcribe DNA sequences into messenger RNA (mRNA)

Identify the criteria that must be satisfied by a region to be designated as a "Biodiversity Hotspot" according to Norman Myers:

A) High level of species richness
B) High degree of endemism
C) Minimal presence of invasive alien species
D) Severe threat of habitat loss (at least 70% of original habitat lost)

### Solution
To qualify as a biodiversity hotspot according to Norman Myers, a region must meet two strict criteria: it must contain at least 1,500 species of vascular plants as endemics (> 0.5% of the world’s total), and it must have lost at least 70% of its original primary vegetation. Thus, species richness, endemism, and high levels of habitat threat are the defining criteria.

In a classic Mendelian monohybrid cross between a homozygous dominant tall pea plant (TT) and a homozygous recessive dwarf pea plant (tt), what is the phenotypic ratio of tall to dwarf plants expected in the F2 generation?

A) 1:1
B) 1:2:1
C) 9:3:3:1
D) 3:1

### Solution
The cross of TT x tt yields all tall heterozygous offspring (Tt) in the F1 generation. Selfing the F1 (Tt x Tt) produces the F2 generation with the genotypic ratio 1 TT : 2 Tt : 1 tt. Phenotypically, TT and Tt are tall, and tt is dwarf, resulting in a 3:1 ratio of tall to dwarf plants.

In a double-stranded DNA molecule, if the percentage of Cytosine bases is 20%, calculate the expected percentage of Adenine bases in the same molecule according to Chargaff's rules.

### Solution
According to Chargaff's rules, in double-stranded DNA:
1. The amount of Cytosine (C) equals the amount of Guanine (G). Since C = 20%, G must also be 20%.
2. The combined percentage of C + G = 20% + 20% = 40%.
3. The remaining percentage belongs to Adenine (A) and Thymine (T): 100% - 40% = 60%.
4. Since A = T, the percentage of Adenine = 60% / 2 = 30%.

Which of the following statements accurately describe the recombinant DNA technology approach used to produce human insulin (Humulin)?

A) The A and B peptide chains are synthesized together as a single proinsulin fusion protein and cleaved later.
B) The DNA sequences corresponding to A and B chains of human insulin are synthesized separately.
C) The separate A and B chains are produced in Escherichia coli and joined subsequently by creating disulfide bonds.
D) The C-peptide is synthesized separately and attached to the A and B chains using DNA ligase.

### Solution
Mature human insulin consists of two short polypeptide chains: chain A and chain B, linked by disulfide bridges. In proinsulin, there is an extra C-peptide which is removed during maturation. Eli Lilly produced Humulin by synthesizing DNA sequences for chains A and B separately, inserting them into plasmids of E. coli to produce chains A and B separately, and then extracting and combining them by creating disulfide bonds in vitro.

The flippers of penguins and dolphins are examples of:

A) Homologous organs representing divergent evolution
B) Analogous organs representing convergent evolution
C) Vestigial organs representing genetic drift
D) Homologous organs representing convergent evolution

### Solution
The flippers of penguins (birds) and dolphins (mammals) have different anatomical origins and structures but perform the same function (swimming/locomotion). This makes them analogous organs, which arise due to convergent evolution in response to similar environmental demands.
