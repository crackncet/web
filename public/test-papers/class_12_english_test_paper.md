Choose the grammatically correct sentence from the options below:

A) Neither the teacher nor the students were present in the hall.
B) Neither the teacher nor the students was present in the hall.
C) Neither the teacher or the students were present in the hall.
D) Neither the teacher nor the students is present in the hall.

### Solution
According to the rule of proximity, when two subjects are joined by "neither... nor," the verb agrees with the subject closest to it. Here, "students" (plural) is closer to the verb, so the plural verb "were" is correct. Also, "neither" must pair with "nor," not "or."

"The chef prepared a delicious meal."

A) A delicious meal is prepared by the chef.
B) A delicious meal was preparing by the chef.
C) A delicious meal was prepared by the chef.
D) A delicious meal prepares the chef.

### Solution

D) She is reading an interesting novel.

Identify the correct indirect speech form of the following direct statement:
She said, "I am writing a letter now."

A) She said that she was writing a letter then.
B) She said that I was writing a letter now.
C) She said that she is writing a letter then.
D) She said that she was writing a letter now.

### Solution
When reporting speech:
1. The pronoun "I" changes to "she".
2. Present continuous "am writing" changes to past continuous "was writing".
3. Adverb of time "now" changes to "then".
Hence, the correct indirect speech is: "She said that she was writing a letter then."
