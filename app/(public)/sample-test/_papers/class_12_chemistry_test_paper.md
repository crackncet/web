Which of the following colligative properties is most suitable for determining the molar masses of polymers, proteins, and other macromolecules?

A) Relative lowering of vapor pressure
B) Osmotic pressure
C) Elevation of boiling point
D) Depression of freezing point

### Solution
Osmotic pressure is preferred for macromolecules because:
1. The pressure measurements are carried out around room temperature, which prevents thermal denaturation of biomolecules.
2. Even for highly dilute polymer solutions, the osmotic pressure values are large enough to be measured with high precision, unlike tiny boiling point elevations or freezing point depressions.

What is the effect of dilution on the specific conductivity ($\kappa$) and molar conductivity ($\Lambda_m$) of an electrolyte solution?

A) Both specific and molar conductivity increase
B) Both specific and molar conductivity decrease
C) Specific conductivity increases, molar conductivity decreases
D) Specific conductivity decreases, molar conductivity increases

### Solution
- **Specific conductivity ($\kappa$)** is the conductance of ions present in a unit volume of solution. Dilution increases the total volume, decreasing the number of current-carrying ions per unit volume, which reduces $\kappa$.
- **Molar conductivity ($\Lambda_m$)** is given by $\Lambda_m = \frac{\kappa \times 1000}{C}$. As dilution increases, concentration $C$ decreases. The decrease in $C$ overrides the decrease in $\kappa$, resulting in an increase in $\Lambda_m$ upon dilution.

For a first-order chemical reaction, if the initial concentration of reactant is doubled, what happens to the half-life ($t_{1/2}$) of the reaction?

A) It remains unchanged
B) It becomes doubled
C) It becomes halved
D) It becomes four times

### Solution
The half-life of a first-order reaction is given by the formula:
$$t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k}$$
where $k$ is the rate constant.
Since the formula does not contain any concentration terms, the half-life is completely independent of the initial concentration of the reactants. Hence, it remains unchanged.

Which of the following elements are transition elements according to the IUPAC definition?

A) Zinc (Zn)
B) Iron (Fe)
C) Copper (Cu)
D) Chromium (Cr)

### Solution
According to IUPAC, a transition element is defined as an element which has an incompletely filled d-subshell in its ground state or in any of its common oxidation states.
- Iron ($3d^6 4s^2$), Chromium ($3d^5 4s^1$), and Copper (which has $3d^9$ in $\text{Cu}^{2+}$) qualify as transition metals.
- Zinc has a completely filled d-subshell ($3d^{10} 4s^2$) in its ground state as well as in its common oxidation state ($\text{Zn}^{2+}$, $3d^{10}$). Hence, Zinc is a d-block element but not a transition element.

What is the hybridization and magnetic behavior of the coordination complex $[Co(NH_3)_6]^{3+}$? (Atomic number of Co = 27)

A) $sp^3d^2$, Paramagnetic
B) $d^2sp^3$, Paramagnetic
C) $d^2sp^3$, Diamagnetic
D) $sp^3d^2$, Diamagnetic

### Solution
In $[Co(NH_3)_6]^{3+}$, Cobalt is in $+3$ oxidation state ($\text{Co}^{3+}$: $3d^6$).
Since $\text{NH}_3$ is a strong field ligand, it forces pairing of the $3d$ electrons, leaving two $3d$ orbitals empty:
$$3d^6 \text{ paired configuration: } (t_{2g})^6 (eg)^0$$
The empty orbitals $2 \times 3d$, $1 \times 4s$, and $3 \times 4p$ hybridize to form six $d^2sp^3$ hybrid orbitals. Since all electrons in the d-subshell are paired, the complex is diamagnetic.

Which of the following alkyl halides undergoes nucleophilic substitution via $\text{S}_{\text{N}}1$ mechanism most rapidly?

A) Tert-butyl chloride
B) Isopropyl chloride
C) Ethyl chloride
D) Methyl chloride

### Solution
The rate of an $\text{S}_{\text{N}}1$ reaction is determined by the stability of the intermediate carbocation formed:
$$\text{Tertiary } (3^\circ) > \text{Secondary } (2^\circ) > \text{Primary } (1^\circ) > \text{Methyl}$$
Tert-butyl chloride forms a highly stable tertiary carbocation due to inductive (+I) effect and hyperconjugation from nine $\alpha$-hydrogens, allowing it to react via $\text{S}_{\text{N}}1$ most rapidly.

If a chemical reaction has a rate law represented by $\text{Rate} = k [A]^1 [B]^1$, what is the overall order of the reaction?

### Solution
The overall order of a chemical reaction is the sum of the exponents of the concentration terms in the rate law equation:
$$\text{Order} = 1 + 1 = 2$$
The reaction is second-order overall.

Which of the following compounds will give a positive Fehling's test?

A) Glucose
B) Acetaldehyde
C) Formaldehyde
D) Benzaldehyde

### Solution
Fehling's test is used to distinguish between reducing aldehydes and non-reducing ones/ketones.
- Aliphatic aldehydes (like acetaldehyde and formaldehyde) and reducing aldose sugars (like glucose) reduce Fehling's solution to form a red precipitate of cuprous oxide ($\text{Cu}_2\text{O}$).
- Benzaldehyde (aromatic aldehyde) does not reduce Fehling's solution due to conjugation of the carbonyl group with the benzene ring.

What is the primary organic product formed when aniline is treated with nitrous acid ($\text{NaNO}_2 + \text{HCl}$) at 0–5 °C?

A) Nitrobenzene
B) Phenol
C) Benzene diazonium chloride
D) Chlorobenzene

### Solution
Treatment of primary aromatic amines (like aniline) with nitrous acid at low temperatures ($0\text{–}5\text{ }^\circ\text{C}$) results in the formation of diazonium salts. This reaction is known as diazotization:
$$\text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_2 + \text{NaNO}_2 + 2\text{HCl} \xrightarrow{0\text{-}5\text{ }^\circ\text{C}} \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{N}_2^+\text{Cl}^- + \text{NaCl} + 2\text{H}_2\text{O}$$
The product is benzene diazonium chloride.
